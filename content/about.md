I'm Somesh Singh — an embedded firmware engineer building secure connected systems on ARM Cortex-M and Cortex-A platforms.

My work covers firmware design and development, Trusted Firmware (TF-M / TF-A) and TrustZone, Zephyr and embedded Linux, secure boot, signed FOTA, hardware Secure Element integration, and the crypto — AES, ECDH, ECC, RSA, TLS — that ties it all together.

I work at Microchip Technology Inc., building secure firmware for IoT, automotive, and EV charging platforms. Recent work contributed to $20M+ in EV Charger reference design wins and cut critical vulnerabilities by 95% through hardware Secure Element-based key provisioning.

## Why this site exists

Most of what's useful in embedded security gets passed around verbally between engineers who've shipped a few products, not written down. This site is an attempt to write some of it down. 

Projects here are personal explorations on publicly available reference platforms — NXP MCXN947, STM32, NVIDIA Jetson — and do not represent the views or work of my employer.
