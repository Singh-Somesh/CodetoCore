About

I’m Somesh Singh — an embedded software engineer specializing in hardware-rooted security for connected devices.

My work focuses on ARM Cortex-M and Cortex-A platforms, Trusted Firmware and TrustZone architectures, and building production-grade reference designs that global OEMs adopt for their own products. In practice, that means secure boot chains, signed FOTA update systems, hardware Secure Element integration, and the symmetric and asymmetric cryptography — AES, ECDH, ECC, RSA, TLS — that holds it all together.

I work at Microchip Technology Inc., where I architect secure firmware for IoT, automotive and EV charging platforms, drive security enablement through Hardware Root of Trust and PKI, and serve as the security subject matter expert across the team. Recent work includes contributing to $20M+ in EV Charger reference design wins, and leading the migration from software certificates to hardware Secure Element-based key provisioning — which delivered a 95% reduction in critical vulnerabilities on the platform.

Why this site exists#

There’s a lot of embedded security content online that’s either vendor marketing or beginner tutorials. Less of it sits in the middle — the kind of content that explains why things are designed a certain way, what the failure modes look like, and what gets glossed over in datasheet claims.

This site is my attempt at that middle space. The posts here cover secure boot, Trusted Firmware (TF-M / TF-A), TrustZone, OP-TEE, Hardware Root of Trust, FOTA security, and the practical realities of building products that hold up under actual threat models.

The projects here are personal explorations on publicly available reference platforms — NXP MCXN947, STM32, and NVIDIA Jetson — and do not represent the views or work of my employer.

What you’ll find here#

  • Writing — long-form technical posts on embedded security, secure boot, FOTA, HRoT, and ARM platform security
  • Projects — open-source explorations of TF-M, OP-TEE, secure boot, and TEE-based architectures on real hardware
